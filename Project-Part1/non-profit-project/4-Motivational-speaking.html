Motivational Speaking

Speficics on Programming:

Public Speaking is never anybody's favourite thing to do. Some people get a lot of training to do so over time. One of our hopes as our clients progress through each part of the programming is that they make the right decisions for themselves in the long term.

There are many aspects to speaking in public, not just giving speaches as most people's anxieties may jump to when they hear those words together.

Even those with natural talents for performance can see speaking as a fear and anxiety inducing activity. Although the title of this section is motivational speaking, this is something to worked towards, rather than to jump right into.

Much of our goal with this programming is to allow youth to gain the confidence in themselves and their abilities, hidden talents or skills that have gove undeveloped to a point where it may be part of the problem. For those who are aging out of care, or getting ready to be released from custody, being job ready, or even interview ready can be a challenge, as many of these facilities do not focus or help with this before release.

Small goals

Depending on participant's level of comfort and engagement so far within a structured environment we will take it as slow as necessary.

Motivational speaking takes time and practice. To get use to a group space we may run similar to a small sharing circle at first. This will allow for a more comfortable space to share and speak in front of others.

  • 1. Introductions

    • Basics: Name, where they came from
    • Telling a bit more about yourself
    • Details: Framing your story, setting it up

  • 2. Icebreakers

    • Creating a space space
    • Finding common ground
    • Making it seem easy

  • 3. Small group discussions

    • Panel discussions
    • Focus groups
    • Peer-support

  • 4. Large group discussions

    • Facilitation practice
    • Conflict resolution
    • Resotortive justice techniques

  • 5. Public Speaking: Sharing your Story

    • How Am I?
    • Where Am I now?
    • Where do I come from?
    • Where am I going?

Milestones

The hope fo the small goals is to reach milestones. These are the points where we can see the progress. Milestsones can be larger goals or even just putting things into practice in a real world setting.

  • Personal Achievements
  • Giving back to community
  • Giving back to family

Breakthroughs

Breakthroughs can happen at any moment. This is the journey or self-work and doing the work creating positive change both for oneself and in the community to a larger extent.

The hope with all our programming, but more specifically the motivational, is that the participants will to share their own story as a healing process, and in turn, be able to help others in the same way. encourgaring each other to be better and do better, not only for themselves, but for the community as a whole.

Interviewing & job skills

This may also be a essential skills to become aware of. Many youth that fall into street life early never learn these skills and may not believe they are even necessary. We may help with the speaking portion at first, how to speak and use body language to effectively market oneself in job situations, Interviewing, as well as networking events.

To Further expand on this idea, we may also introduce basic trade skills at some point. Down the road, we are hoping to secure our own location. In the event of this happening, we might even be able to conduct small builds on the property. For youth to be included we'd like to introduce basics one tool usage and possibly entry trade skills that may allow them to get into an apprenticeship.

Interviewing for media

As many talented young people may be coming through this programming to learn how to work in the studio, write their own music an lyrics, and get involved in the various spaces where artists mingle with general public and by extension possible media interactions, we see this as a neccessity. People in general, but moreso youth, speak differently among their peer group, and then again with parents and teachers. To be an artist at times, means you may also need to conduct yourself as a professional. This may mean speaking to media, or even just being in a public space where you may be asked questions about your art, or your process. We want to ensure that our youth are prepared for these types of interactions. This could be critical to the success of a piece or an album, or even a career. One on one interviews, panel discussions, or even dealing with random fans or critics without preparation can be a daunting task.

Motivational Speaking for youth

One of our tenets of this youth programmming is the "Pay it Forward" mentality. We did not invent it, but we have chosen to employ it because it allows for us and the youth going through this type of programming to give back to other youth and community as a whole. This will effectively "Build Our Village," and engage youth in converation with others who may be at risk, or displaying signs of things that may lead to rishy choices. We also heavliy rely on the Seven Grandfather Teachings as a way of life and a positive way to walk one's path to make better choices going forward.

Interacting with Law enforcement

We think it is very important to have these conversations. Interacting with Law enforcement can have consequences and repercussions depending on how the interaction takes place, proceeds, and progresses throughout. Many young people think it may not be a big deal, especially if they have not been in trouble beforehand, however, things can go wrong at the slightest hint of provocation or misunderstanding. We want to ensure that our youth get through these encounters safely and effeciently with the least amount of follow-up or record taking as possible. The Idea and hope for this focus on speaking, is to know, not only your rights in these situations, but to also understand that they may not matter at times, and effectively navigating how to conduct oneself in order to come away safely. Some people know this to called "The Talk."
